What is The Path?

An Initiation,
Not a Course

The Rose Goddess Path is a living, breathing initiation into the full spectrum of the feminine. Over 10 weeks, you will journey through 5 sacred Stages — each one a death and a rebirth — and embody 7 Archetypes of the Rose lineage.

This is not about learning something new. It is about remembering something ancient. It is about coming home to yourself — all of yourself — in the deepest possible way.

The journey begins with the Rose Dieta, a 2-week initiation that prepares your body, spirit, and field to receive the medicine of the Rose.

The Beginning

The Rose Dieta

Before entering the full initiation, every woman undergoes the Rose Dieta — a 2-week ceremonial preparation period. This is a time of intentional simplification, cleansing, and opening. You will be guided through specific practices, dietary adjustments, and ceremonial work to prepare your entire being to receive the deep medicine of the Rose Goddess Path.

The Dieta is not a prerequisite to be completed — it is the first act of devotion. The first place you practice saying yes to yourself.

"Before the rose blooms, she must be tended. The Dieta is how we tend the soil of your soul."

— Rosa Diaz de Leon

The Journey

5 Stages of
the Rose

Each stage is a complete world — an element, an archetype, a death, a rebirth. You will not leave any stage the same woman who entered it.

I
Earth
Awakening
the Body

The foundation of all healing is the body. In this first stage, you return to your physical home — meeting your nervous system, your sensations, your hungers. You begin to remember that your body is not a problem to be solved, but a temple to be inhabited. The earth holds you. You learn to let it.

Archetype
The Seed
II
Water
Reclaiming
Pleasure

Pleasure is not a luxury — it is your birthright. In the Water stage, you reclaim your erotic innocence, your sensuality, your capacity for delight. You meet the conditioning that taught you to shrink, to perform, to apologize for your desire. And you begin, gently, to dissolve it.

Archetype
The Bud
III
Fire
Shadow
Integration

The fire does not destroy — it refines. In this stage, you descend into the shadow: the rage, the grief, the shame, the parts of yourself you have hidden from the world and from yourself. You meet the Thorn Keeper — the fierce guardian of your most sacred wounds. And you discover that she has been protecting gold.

Archetype
The Thorn Keeper
IV
Air
Reclaiming
Your Voice

Your voice is your sovereignty. In the Air stage, you reclaim the right to speak your truth — to name your needs, to set your boundaries, to be heard. You meet the Wild Rose: untamed, unashamed, wholly herself. You practice living from that place.

Archetype
The Wild Rose
V
Spirit
Rose Goddess
Integration

The final stage is not an arrival — it is an integration. You gather everything you have met, healed, and reclaimed, and you bring it fully into your life. You embody the Rose Goddess: whole, sovereign, radiant, and real. You know, now, who you are. And you know how to live from there.

Archetype
The Rose Goddess

The 7 Archetypes

Faces of the
Rose Lineage

Each archetype is a facet of your wholeness — a part of you waiting to be recognized, embodied, and integrated.

I

The Seed

Dormant potential. The untouched self before conditioning. The pure essence of who you were before the world told you who to be.

II

The Bud

Tender emergence. The self beginning to open. Soft, curious, reaching toward the light with cautious hope.

III

The Blooming Rose

Radiant expression. Fully open, fully herself, unashamed of her beauty and fragrance. The self in full creative flow.

IV

The Thorn Keeper

Sacred protector. The fierce guardian of your boundaries and your wounds. She does not apologize for her edges — they are holy.

V

The Wild Rose

Untamed sovereignty. She who grows where she will, answers to no one, and speaks her truth without apology or explanation.

VI

Priestess of the Rose

Sacred service. She who tends the temple, holds the ceremony, carries the lineage. Devotion as a way of life.

VII

The Rose Goddess

Full integration. The woman who has walked through all the stages, met all the faces of herself, and emerged whole. Not perfect — whole. Not healed — healing. Fully alive. Fully here. Fully herself.
